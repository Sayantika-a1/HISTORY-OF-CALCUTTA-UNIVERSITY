The Bygone Days

The University of Calcutta (CU) was founded on January 24, 1857, in pursuance of a dispatch from the Court of Directors of the East India Company to the Governor-General of India in Council in 1854. The dispatch suggested the establishment of universities in Calcutta, Bombay, and Madras. CU is the oldest multidisciplinary university in the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, and is considered the first Western-style institution of its kind in Asia. It is accredited as an "A" grade university by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC).

On the whole

Its alumni and faculty include several heads of state and government, social reformers, prominent artists, the only Indian Dirac Medal winner, many Fellows of the Royal Society and six Nobel laureates who are Ronald Ross, Rabindranath Tagore, C. V. Raman, Amartya Sen, and Abhijit Banerjee.The university has the highest number of students who have cleared the National Eligibility Test. The University of Calcutta is a member of the United Nations Academic Impact.
-By Sayantika Das
